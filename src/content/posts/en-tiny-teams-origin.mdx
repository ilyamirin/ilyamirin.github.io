Tiny teams are often presented as a new AI-era idea. I think that is too shallow.

The deeper pattern is older: small autonomous groups of strong generalists can solve hard problems with less friction than larger structures, especially when trust and ownership are high.

AI changes the economics because some analysis, support, prototyping, and automation can now be pushed into model-assisted systems.

So the principle is old. The leverage is new.
