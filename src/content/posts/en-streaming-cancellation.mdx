Streaming cancellation is easy to underestimate until the bills become real.

If the client stops rendering tokens but the server keeps decoding, the product only *looks* cancelled. The infrastructure knows better.

That is why stop is not just a UX action. It is an architectural boundary that should travel through transport, orchestration, and provider inference.

Good LLM systems are defined partly by how quickly and cleanly they can stop.
